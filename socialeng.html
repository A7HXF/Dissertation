Social Engineering

Understanding Social Engineering Attacks

These tactics involve manipulating individuals into breaking security protocols to access confidential information.

What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering exploits human psychology, rather than technical hacking techniques, to gain access to buildings, systems, or data.

Main Issues Caused by Social Engineering

1. Unauthorised Access: Perpetrators gain entry to restricted areas or systems, leading to data breaches.

2. Loss of Sensitive Information: Critical information such as passwords and IDs can be manipulated out of employees or customers.

3. System Compromise: Entire networks can be endangered due to manipulated insider actions.

4. Reputation Damage: Companies can suffer severe reputational harm affecting customer trust and business operations.

Examples of Social Engineering

1. Pretexting: Crafting a good pretext, or a fabricated scenario, to engage a targeted victim.

2. Baiting: Offering something enticing to an end-user, in exchange for login credentials or private information.

3. Phishing: Sending fake communications that appear to come from reputable sources, often directing users to enter personal information at a fake website.

Prevention Tips for Social Engineering

To protect yourself from Social Engineering Attacks, follow these essential steps:

1. Be Skeptical: Question the legitimacy of unexpected requests for sensitive information, even if they seem to come from within the company.

2. Verify Identities: Always confirm the identity of the person requesting information, especially if sensitive data is involved.

3. Educate Employees: Conduct regular training sessions to teach employees about the risks of social engineering and how to avoid them.

4. Secure Access Points: Implement strict policies and procedures for accessing sensitive data and areas within the organization.
